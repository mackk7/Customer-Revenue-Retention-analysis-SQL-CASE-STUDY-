# Customer-Revenue-Retention-analysis-SQL-CASE-STUDY-

## Purpose
Use SQL to analyze customer buying behavior, revenue trends, and retention patterns for business decision-making.

## Data
Simulated but behaviorally accurate transactional data with:
- customers
- orders
- order_items

Data is simulated to reflect real-world buying patterns such as repeat business, category leadership, payment methods, and revenue distribution.

## Business Questions Answered
- What is the total and monthly revenue trend?
- Which customers generate the most revenue?
- How much revenue is generated by repeat vs. single customers?
- How well is customer retention over time?
- What is the month-over-month revenue change?
- Which product categories and cities generate the most revenue?
- Is there a small set of customers who generate a disproportionate share of revenue (Pareto analysis)?

## Analysis Insights
- Revenue is primarily generated by repeat customers, not single customers.
- A small set of customers generates a disproportionate share of total revenue.
- There are periods of revenue growth and stagnation on a month-over-month basis.
- Retention analysis shows areas for improvement in customer loyalty.
- Electronics and Fashion product categories lead in overall revenue.

## Skills Utilized
- SQL joins and aggregations
- SQL window functions (lag, row_number, rank)
- Time series and cohort analysis
- Customer analysis (retention, CLV, repeat business)
- Business-focused insights development

## Tools Used
- PostgreSQL
- pgAdmin
